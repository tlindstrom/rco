了

HSK Level: 1

The usage of 了 is a huge topic in the Chinese language because we simply could not omit this word from our conversations, and its meanings are very diverse and can be very subtle. All of this could make learning 了 a nightmare! Here, I will try to explain all the possible meanings of 了 .

Perfective aspect marker

We can use 了 to mark that an event is completed. Many learners get confused about the usage of 了 and 了 . Indeed, they can both be related to past events, but they cannot be interchanged without an alteration of meaning. When we talk about past-events, 了 emphasizes whether an event is done/completed, while 過 (guò) emphasizes whether an event has ever been experienced before. To learn more about 過 (guò), click here. On this page, we will focus on the usage of 了 (le).
Verb + 了 + Object

我 殺 了 她 。
wǒ shā le tā 。
I killed her.
我 昨天 吃 了 壽司 。
wǒ zuótiān chī le shòusī 。
I ate sushi yesterday.
我 打破 了 你的 花瓶 。
wǒ dǎpò le nǐde huāpíng 。
I broke your vase.

Since 了(le) is an aspect marker, it gives information that an event is completed , but not when the event takes place. Therefore, it can also be used in future events. For example:

我 明天 吃 了 午飯 後 ， 要 去 醫院 。
wǒ míngtiān chī le wǔfàn hòu ， yào qù yīyuàn 。
I need to go to the hospital tomorrow after I have finished lunch.
他 來到 的 時候 ， 我 已經 餓 死 了 。
tā láidào de shíhòu ， wǒ yǐjīng è sǐ le 。
By the time he arrives, I will have already starved to death.

Change marker

了 (le) is also used to emphasize when a new situation or a change has occurred.
For example:

我 喜歡 了 你 。
wǒ xǐhuān le nǐ 。
I have fallen for you．

(了 (le) marks the change in psychological state from ‘not liking’ to ‘liking’.)

我 高 了 。
wǒ gāo le 。
I have grown taller.

(Here, 了 (le) marks the change of height.)

我 妹妹 現在 吃 肉 了 。
wǒ mèimei xiànzài chī ròu le 。
My sister eats meat now.

(了 (le) here suggests that the sister did not eat meat before but has now started eating it.)

我 現在 十八 歲 了 ！
wǒ xiànzài shíbā suì le ！
I am 18 years old already!

(Here, 'I' emphasize that I am ALREADY an adult, no longer a kid.'I' probably want to be treated as an adult now.)

Emphatic particle

We also use 了 (le) to emphasize the quality or quantity of something. Sometimes 了 (le) is required in a sentence when there is an intensifying adverb, such as 太 (tài), 多 (duō) and 可 (kě). Therefore, the following examples sound much more authentic and natural with 了 (le) at the end.

這 個 蘋果 太 貴 了 ！
zhè ge pínguǒ tài guì le ！
This apple is too expensive!
我的 男朋友 對 我 太 好 了 ！
wǒde nánpéngyǒu duì wǒ tài hǎo le ！
My boyfriend treats me too well!
這裡 的 酒 便宜 多 了 ！
zhèlǐ de jiǔ biànyi duō le ！
The alcoholic drinks here are way cheaper!
我的 電腦 比 你的 快 多 了 ！
wǒde diànnǎo bǐ nǐde kuài duō le ！
My computer is way faster than yours!
這 個 女人 可 醜 了 ！
zhè ge nǚrén kě chǒu le ！
This woman is really ugly!
他的 老婆 可 嚴 了 ！
tāde lǎopó kě yán le ！
His wife is really strict!

We can also use 了 (le) to emphasize that something has already occurred, most likely related to the time of the speech. This is quite similar to the usage of present/past perfect tense; when 了 (le) does not have such an emphatic effect, it will more likely make a sentence with a simple past tense. In the examples below, both of the second instances of 了 (le) are used as an emphatic particle:

我 已經 做好 了 功課 了 ， 我 現在 可以 出去 嗎 ？
wǒ yǐjīng zuòhǎo le gōngkè le ， wǒ xiànzài kěyǐ chūqù ma ？
I have done my homework already, can I go out now?
她 喝 了 十 杯 了 ， 不要 再 逼 她 喝 了 ！
tā hē le shí bēi le ， bùyào zài bī tā hē le ！
She has drunk 10 glasses already, stop forcing her to drink more!

We have three uses of 了 (le) in the second example. The first one is used to express that the event of finishing ten glasses was completed. The second one is used to stress that she has drunk a lot already, so she should not drink more. The third one is used to make the imperative more gentle, which we will discuss further now.

Tone softening particle

As ridiculous as it might sound to you, 了 (le) can be used to tone down an imperative. Quite a departure from its use as an emphatic particle, which we talked about just now! If we want to sound less forceful when making an imperative, we can add 了 (le) at the end of it. The following imperatives become more gentle with 了 (le):

別 笑 我 了 。
bié xiào wǒ le 。
Don't laugh at me.
別再 說話 了 。
biézài shuōhuà le 。
Stop talking.
大家 都 聽好 了 。
dàjiā dōu tīnghǎo le 。
Please listen.

Note that speakers always have a mixture of feelings and thoughts in their mind, so it is possible that 了 (le) is used for several purposes at the same time. For example, in 下大雨了, 了 could be used as a perfective marker, change marker and emphatic particle at the same time. Because of the great number of functions 了 (le) has and how they are somewhat correlated, many learners find learning this particle notoriously challenging. Indeed, it could be difficult to use 了 (le) yourself in the beginning but I hope this article has helped you ease at least some of your confusion!

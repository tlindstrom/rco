要

HSK Level: 2

The Chinese word 要 (yào) is a widely used verb which have several meanings: to want, to need and to be going to. Sometimes it is obvious which meaning 要 (yào) refers to but it is also quite often that we find grey areas where more than one single meaning can apply to a sentence. In this article, we will go through each of its possible meanings and you will understand why such a 'grey area' exists.
要 (yào) can be followed by a noun or a verb:
要 + noun/verb

要 (yào) as to want

你 要 做 什麼 ？
nǐ yào zuò shénme ？
What do you want to do?
我 要 去 海邊 ！
wǒ yào qù hǎibiān !
I want to go to the beach!
我 要 李嘉誠 的 薪金 。
wǒ yào lǐjiāchéng de xīnjīn 。
I want the salary of Li Ka Shing.
我 要 結實 的 肌肉 。
wǒ yào jiēshí de jīròu 。
I want firm muscles.

Note that there is another word in Chinese that also means to want: 想 (xiǎng) . 要 (yào) comes across as firmer than 想 (xiǎng).

要 (yào) as to need

要 (yào) can also be used to express our needs. Sometimes it also contains the meaning to want because it is very possible that we want something as much as we feel like we need it.
Let's have some examples again:

十一 點 了 ， 我 現在 要 回家 了 。
shíyī diǎn le ， wǒ xiànzài yào huíjiā le 。
It's already 11 o'clock, I need to go home now.
我 媽媽 病 了 ， 我 要 去 探望 她 。
wǒ māma bìng le ， wǒ yào qù tànwàng tā 。
My mum is sick, I need to visit her.
湯姆 要 食物 ， 他 快 餓死 了 。
tāngmǔ
Tom needs food, he is starving.
去 中國 要 乘 火車 。
qù zhōngguó yào chéng huǒchē 。
We need to take a train to go to China.

要 (yào) as to be going to

We can also use 要 (yào) for future events. Indeed, it overlaps the definition to want or to need as there must be certain extent of desire or need if we are going to do something in the future. However, if 要 (yào) signifies a future event, it makes sense to say that it is similar to be going to in English.
In this case, 要 (yào) can only be followed by a verb:
要 + verb
Here are some examples:

我 明天 要 跟 總統 共進 晚餐 。
wǒ míngtiān yào gēn zǒngtǒng gòngjìn wǎncān 。
I am going to have dinner with the president.
總統 下 一 年 要 離職 。
zǒngtǒng xià yī nián yào lízhí 。
The president is going to resign next year.
每個人 最終 都 要 死 。
měigerén zuìzhōng dōu yào sǐ 。
Everyone is going to die in the end.
嬰兒 要 哭 了 。
yīngér yào kū le 。
The baby is going to cry.

In the above examples, we can see that it could be quite difficult to have a clear-cut definition for 要 (yào) . Like in the last example, babies are going to cry most of the time because they want to unless they have been slapped to do so; also, sometimes babies cry because they think they need to in order to let their parents know that they are hungry.

The negative 不要 (bùyào)

I think all of you have grasped the above content pretty well, let's move on to talk about the negative 不要 (bùyào). It is quite similar to don't in English. In Chinese, many commands are done by adding 不要 (bùyào) before a verb:
不要 + verb
The examples below are commands with 不要 (bùyào):

不要 餵 我的 狗 ！
bùyào wèi wǒde gǒu ！
Don't feed my dog!
不要 叫 我 「 美女 」 ！
bùyào jiào wǒ 「 měinǚ 」 ！
Don't call me 'beautiful chick'!
不要 撒謊 ， 不然的話 …
bùyào sāhuǎng ， bùrándehuà …
Don't lie to me, otherwise...
不要 覺得 你 自己 是 公主 ， 好 嗎 ？
bùyào juéde nǐ zìjǐ shì gōngzhǔ ， hǎo ma ？
Don't think that you are a princess, ok?

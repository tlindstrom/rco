很

HSK Level: 1

很 is often translated as “very” but can also be used more generally. One of its primary functions is to link a subject to an adjective.

Linking subjects to descriptions with 很

n. + 很 + adj.

Essentially, 很 acts as a de facto form of “to be” that can only be used to link a subject to an adjective. To link a subject to another noun, use 是 instead.

我 媽媽 很 年輕 。
Wǒ māmā hěn niánqīng.
My mom is very young.
你的 朋友 很 好 聊 。
Nǐde péngyǒu hěn hǎo liáo.
Your friend is nice to chat with.
睡覺 很 健康 。
Shuìjiào hěn jiànkāng.
Sleep is healthy.
他 平時 說話 很 謹慎 ， 我 也 不 知道 為什麼 他 會 這樣 說 。
Tā píngshí shuōhuà hěn jǐnshèn. Wǒ yě bù zhīdào wéishénme tā huì zhèyàng shuō.
He is usually careful with his words, I don't know why he would say this either.

When 很 means “very”

很 + adj. + 的+ n.

In some situations, 很 actually does mean “very”. For instance, descriptions are often connected to the front of a noun with 的, like 快樂 的 兔子 , which means “happy rabbit”. In cases like this, we can add 很 to mean “very”.

很 快樂 的 兔子
hěn kuàilè de tùzǐ
very happy rabbit
很 勤奮 的 員工
hěn qínfèn de yuángōng
very hardworking employee
他 是 個 很 主動 的 學生 。
Tā shì ge hěn zhǔdòng de xuéshēng.
He is a very proactive student.
書 裡 有 很 多 很 有趣 的 故事 。
Shū lǐ yǒu hěnduō hěn yǒuqù de gùshì.
There are so many very interesting stories in the book.

Showing degree while linking a subject to an opinion-based adjective

n. +很 + adj.

If an adjective can either be an objective fact or a subjective description, there are two different ways to connect it to the noun. If it’s an objective fact, we use 是 . . . 的. If it’s subjective, we use 很.

玻璃 是 透明 的 。
Bōlí shì tòumíng de.
Glass is transparent.

It’s just a fact. Glass is transparent. But with 很 . . .

這 張 玻璃 很 透明 ！
Zhè zhāng bōlí hěn tòumíng!
This sheet of glass is very transparent!

The speaker is stating his opinion on how incredibly transparent the glass is. Perhaps it was cleaned recently.

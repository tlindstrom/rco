就

HSK Level: 2

就(jiù) may seem quite complicated to learners since it has several different, rather subtle uses. It has the following five main functions:
1) to express 'small in quantity' or 'uniqueness'
2) to express that something happens very 'early'
3) to signal a sequence between two events
4) to give an emphatic statement
5) to give a contrasting statement
Let's go through them one by one now.

To express 'small in quantity' or 'uniqueness'

When the subject is unique/few, we put 就 (jiù) before it.
就 + subject noun
For example:

就 我 一 個 有 孩子 。
jiù wǒ yī ge yǒu háizi 。
Only I have children.
全部 人 都 討厭 他 ， 就 我 喜歡 他 。
quánbù rén dōu tǎoyàn tā ， jiù wǒ xǐhuān tā 。
Only I like him. Everyone else hates him.
就 她們 幾 個 想 留下 。
jiù tāmen jǐ ge xiǎng liúxià 。
Only those few girls wanted to stay.

When an object is unique/few, we put 就 (jiù) before the verb of the object.
就 + verb + object
For example:

我 媽媽 就 會 做 幾 道 菜 。
wǒ māma zhǐ huì zuò jǐ dào cài 。
My mum can only cook a few dishes.
哥哥 就 買 了 幾 條 香蕉 。
gēge jiù mǎi le jǐ tiáo xiāngjiāo 。
My brother has only bought a few bananas.
唐老 就 有 一 個 老婆 ？
tánglǎo jiù yǒu yī ge lǎopó ？
Donald has only one wife?

To function as a verb phrase ‘only have’

就 + object
這裡 就 一 個 人 ？
zhèlǐ jiù yī ge rén ？
There is only one person in here?
我 就 三 塊 錢 。
wǒ jiù sān kuài qián 。
I only have 3 dollars.
我 就 五 個 男朋友 。
wǒ jiù wǔ ge nánpéngyǒu 。
I only have five boyfriends.

To express that something happens really soon / early

就 + verb
For example:

她 一 歲 就 能 游泳 。
tā yī suì jiù néng yóuyǒng 。
She can already swim at the age of one.
我 現在 就 來 。
wǒ xiànzài jiù lái 。
I will come over right now.
我 五 點鐘 就 起床 。
wǒ wǔ diǎnzhōng jiù qǐchuáng 。
I've been up since 5 a.m.!

To signal a sequence between two events

First event + 就 + second event
For example:

這 隻 狗 看見 我 就 笑 。
zhè zhī gǒu kànjiàn wǒ jiù xiào 。
The dog smiles as soon as he sees me.
這 個 孩子 一 罵 就 哭 。
zhè ge háizi yī mà jiù kū 。
This child cries immediately after she gets scolded.
我 起床 後 就 工作 。
wǒ qǐchuáng hòu jiù gōngzuò 。
I'll start work once I get up.

To make an emphatic statement

You might find the following conversation familiar:
(The girlfriend is mad at the boyfriend for some reason.)

男朋友 ： 我們 去 看 電影 ， 好嗎 ？
nánpéngyǒu ： wǒmen qù kàn diànyǐng ， hǎoma ？
Boyfriend: Let's go for a movie, shall we?
女朋友 ： 我 不 去 。
nǚpéngyǒu ： wǒ bù qù 。
Girlfriend: No.
男朋友 ： 去 吧 。
nánpéngyǒu ： qù ba 。
Boyfriend: Please come with me.
女朋友 ： 我 就 不 去 ！
nǚpéngyǒu ： wǒ jiù bù qù ！
Girlfriend: NO!

To contrast statements

For example:

瑞士 很 貴 ， 德國 相比之下 就 便宜 多 了 。
ruìshì hěn guì ， bólín xiāngbǐzhīxià jiù biànyi duō le 。
Switzerland is very expensive, while Germany is way cheaper.
她 喜歡 打 籃球 ， 我 就 喜歡 踢 足球 。
tā xǐhuān dǎ lánqiú ， wǒ jiù xǐhuān tī zúqiú 。
She likes to play basketball, whereas I like to play football.

All these sound a bit complicated, right? Don't worry! It's always difficult to learn from mere paper talk, the more you read or talk with native speakers, the more confidence you will have in using this complicated particle!

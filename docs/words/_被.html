被

HSK Level: 3

被 (bèi) can be used as a passive preposition in a passive sentence or or as quilt in English.

被 as a passive preposition

Just like in English, we have passive voice in Chinese as well. There is more than one single way to construct a passive sentence but one of the most common ways is to use the passive preposition 被 (bèi) .
When we use 被 (bèi) in a passive sentence, we re-order the elements within the sentence, which is actually quite similar to English.
In a 被-passive sentence, we have the following structure:
action recipient + 被 + action doer + verb
(The action doer in Chinese passive is optional, same as in English.)
Examples:

我的 錢包 被 一 個 陌生人 偷 了 。
wǒde qiánbāo bèi yī gè mòshēngrén tōu le 。
My purse got stolen by a stranger.
學校 被 政府 拆卸 了 。
xuéxiào bèi zhèngfǔ chāixiè le 。
The school got demolished by the government.
我 被 你 弄 瘋 了 。
wǒ bèi nǐ nòng fēng le 。
I have been driven crazy by you!
湖 被 有毒 的 化學物質 污染 了 。
hú bèi yǒudú de huàxuéwùzhì wūrǎn le 。
The lake was polluted by hazardous chemicals.

Restrictions of 被

The 被-passive sentences are less common than English passive sentences because the usage of 被 (bèi) is restricted by two additional conditions:
1) it must be used to express adversity
2) action recipients must be affected by the action
We'll now go through these two conditions one by one:

Must be used to express adversity

被 (bèi) has a negative connotation so we can only use it for unfortunate or unpleasant events. The following examples sound off because 被 (bèi) is used for events that are supposed to be positive:

他 被 老師 獎賞 了 。
tā bèi lǎoshī jiǎngshǎng le 。
我們的 生活 素質 被 提升 了 。
wǒmen de shēnghuó sùzhì bèi tíshēng le
發展中國家 被 已發展國家 幫助 了 。
fāzhǎnzhōng guójiā bèi yǐfāzhǎn guójiā bāngzhù le 。
這 部 儀器 被 他 修好 了 。
zhè bù yíqì bèi tā xiūbǔ le 。

Actually, we can see a lot of native speakers use 被 (bèi) in positive events. The increase in usage is thought to be due to the influence of English, in which passive voice is used more often. Therefore, it is now more-or-less acceptable to use 被 (bèi) in positive events in the sense that people can still understand you. However, in proper Chinese grammar, 被 (bèi) is only used to express adversity. When it comes to neutral events, using 被 (bèi) will impose a pejorative meaning on them and turn them into a negative event. Here are some examples:

我的 前度 被 他 娶 了 ， 真的 完 了 。
wǒde qiándù bèi tā qǔ le ， zhēnde wán le 。
That guy married my ex, I guess it really is over!
咖啡 已經 被 他 喝 了 ， 我 還 能 喝 什麼 呢 ？
kāfēi yǐjīng bèi tā hē le ， wǒ huán néng hē shénme ne ？
He already drank all the coffee! What else can I drink then?
最 後 一 條 裙子 已經 被 買 了 。
zuì hòu yī tiáo qúnzi yǐjīng bèi mǎi le 。
The last dress has already been bought.

There are other instances in which 被-passive voice will turn neutral toned sentence into negative ones. Certain neutral verbs of perception or cognition, such as 發現 fāxiàn, 看見 kànjiàn and 聽見 tīngjiàn, are given a negative meaning when used together with 被 (bèi). When they are used in a 被-passive sentence, it implies that something should not be perceived and therefore the event that 'something being perceived' is bad.
Here are some examples to explain this usage:

秘密 被 發現 了 。
mìmì bèi fāxiàn le 。
Th secret has been discovered.
他們的 對話 被 聽見 了 。
tāmen duìhuà bèi tīngjiàn le 。
Their conversation has been overheard.
總統 被 看見 與 一 個 年輕 的 女人 一起 。
zǒngtǒng bèi kànjiàn yǔ yī gè niánqīng de nǚrén yīqǐ 。
The president has been seen being with a young woman.

Action recipients must be affected by the action

In a 被-passive, the action recipient must be affected to some extent by the action. Like in the above example, the president would definitely be affected after being seen with a woman. He could be be criticized by the public, or his wife might file for a divorce. In other words, the recipient must be handled in some way and some effect must be made on the object, which is quite similar to the disposal meaning of 把 (bǎ) .
The examples below are not grammatically correct because the action recipients are not affected at all:

意見 被 我 不 同意 了 。
yìjiàn bèi wǒ bù tóngyì le 。
這 首 歌 被 我 聽 了 。
zhè shǒu gē bèi wǒ tīng le 。
話 被 我 說 了 。
huà bèi wǒ shuō le 。
我 朋友 被 我 恨 。
wǒ péngyǒu bèi wǒ hèn 。

Unfortunately, no matter how much you hate a person, s/he won't be affected at all unless you use the hate to fuel further action.

被 as quilt

我 媽媽 會 在 晚上 走到 我 房間 看 我 有 沒 有 蓋 被 。
wǒ māma huì zài wǎnshàng zǒudào wǒ fángjiān kàn wǒ yǒu méi yǒu gài bèi 。
My mum would come to my room during the night to make sure I was under my quilt.
我 冬天 需要 一 張 羊毛 被 。
wǒ dōngtiān xūyào yī zhāng yángmáo bèi 。
I need a woollen quilt for winter.

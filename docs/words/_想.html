想

HSK Level: 1

想 (xiǎng) can be translated to English as either would like to, to miss or to think. Since they have exactly the same pronunciation, we can only get its correct meaning from their context. Let's look in detail how 想 (xiǎng) can produce these three meanings.

想 (xiǎng) as would like to

It is similar to 要 (yào) , but less emphatic. It should always be followed by a verb like in the following:
subject + 想 + verb

你 想 喝 什麼 ？
nǐ xiǎng hē shénme ?
What would you like to drink?
我 想 跟 您 工作 。
wǒ xiǎng gēn nín gōngzuò 。
I would like to work for you.
我 想 邀請 你 今晚 一起 吃 晚餐 。
wǒ xiǎng yāoqǐng nǐ jīnwǎn yīqǐ chī wǎncān 。
I would like to invite you for dinner tonight.
我 很 想 親 他 ！
wǒ hěn xiǎng qīn tā ！
I would really like to kiss him!

As mentioned above, 想 (xiǎng) is very similar to 要 (yào) , but it is more polite. We can also pair them together in the same sentence as 想要 (xiǎngyào), which means to want.
When put together, they can be followed by either a verb or a noun:
subject + 想要 + verb/noun

我 想要 一 隻 猫 ！
wǒ xiǎngyào yī zhī māo ！
I want a cat!
我 想要 成為 一 個 百萬富翁 ！
wǒ xiǎng chéngwéi yī ge bǎiwànfùwēng ！
I want to be a millionaire!
我 想要 環遊 世界 。
wǒ xiǎngyào huányóu shìjiè 。
I want to travel around the world.

Now that we have learnt that 想 (xiǎng) is used to express our desires, let's move on to see how it is used as to miss.

想 (xiǎng) as to miss

想 (xiǎng) must be put before a noun in a sentence:
subject + 想 + object
Missing someone can be a bad feeling! Let's have a look at some sad but true examples:

我 想 她 ， 但 她 想 他 。
wǒ xiǎng tā ， dàn tā xiǎng tā 。
I miss her but she misses him.
我 想 死 他 了 ！ 他 什麼 時候 會 回來 ？
wǒ xiǎng sǐ tā le ！ tā shénme shíhòu huì huílái ？
I miss him so much! When will he come back?

Now you know one more thing in Chinese to tell your boyfriend/girlfriend. Before you go and practice, let's learn the final meaning: to think.

想 (xiǎng) as to think

When 想 (xiǎng) is followed by a clause, it can be translated as to think; when it is followed by a noun, it means to think of.
So it can precede either a clause or a noun:
subject + 想 + clause/noun
The following is some examples again:

我 想 他 醉 了 。
wǒ xiǎng tā zuì le 。
I think he is drunk.
我 想 他 還 在 家 。
wǒ xiǎng tā hái zài jiā 。
I think he is still at home.
我 連 在 工作 的 時候 ， 也 在 想 他 。
wǒ lián zài gōngzuò de shíhòu ， yě zài xiǎng tā 。
I think of him even when I am at work.
別 只 坐 在 那裡 ， 想想 新 方法 吧 ！
bié zhǐ zhù zài nàlǐ ， xiǎngxiang xīn fāngfǎ ba ！
Stop just sitting in there, think of a new solution!

Practice

Practice makes perfect! The example below contains three instances of 想 (xiǎng) with with its three different meanings, respectively. Without looking at the English translation, can you figure out their meanings from context?

我 想 ， 她 真的 很 想 她 媽媽 ， 但 她 不 應 整天 也 在 想 她 ， 她 得 活 在 當下 。
wǒ xiǎng ， tā zhēnde hěn xiǎng tā māma ， dàn tā bù yīng zhěngtiān yě zài xiǎng tā ， tā děi huó zài dāngxià 。
I think she really misses her mother, but she shouldn't be thinking of her all the time. She should live in the moment!

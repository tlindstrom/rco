你好

HSK Level: 1

你好! Hope you are enjoying learning Chinese. 你好 (nǐhǎo) is used to greet people and means hello. It literally means you are well. Let's look at some examples with 你好 (nǐhǎo):

你好 ， 有 訂座 嗎 ？
nǐhǎo ， yǒu dìngzuò ma ？
Hello, do you have a reservation?
你好 ， 我 叫 李名 。
nǐhǎo ， wǒ jiào lǐmíng 。
Hi, my name is Li Ming.

For a formal conversation, we can change the informal 你 (nǐ) into formal 您 (nín) and use 您好 (nínhǎo) instead. Here are some examples:

您好 ， 我 是 這裡 的 經理 。
nínhǎo ， wǒ shì zhèlǐ de jīnglǐ 。
Hi, I am the manager here.
您好 ， 我 是 您的 學生 。
nínhǎo ， wǒ shì nínde xuéshēng 。
Hi, I am your student.

When we add the question marker 嗎 (ma) after 你好 (nǐhǎo) , as in 你好嗎 (nǐhǎo ma) , we produce the meaning how are you. 你好嗎 (nǐhǎo ma) literally means are you well, so we actually ask whether someone is doing well rather than how someone is doing. Same as 你好 (nǐhǎo) , it is very simple to use 你好嗎 (nǐhǎo ma) . For example:

你好 ， 你好嗎 ？
nǐhǎo ， nǐhǎoma ？
Hi, how are you?
好 久 沒 見 了 ， 你好嗎 ？
hǎo jiǔ méi jiàn le ， nǐhǎoma ？
Haven't seen you for ages, how are you?

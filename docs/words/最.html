最

HSK Level: 2

最 (zuì) is used to mark superlatives. Along with marking adjectives, it can also be used to mark some psychological verbs, such as to hate and to miss. The structure for superlatives is very straightforward and consistent. We simply put 最 (zuì) before an adjective or a psychological verb. (YES! Chinese is finally easier than English... here.)
So, if we want to build a superlative, we can simply follow the structure below:
最 + adjective/ stative verb
Let's look at some examples of both superlative adjectives and superlative psychological verbs.

Adjective superlatives ##

俄羅斯是世界上最大的國家。
éluósī shì shìjiè shàng zuì dà de guójiā .
Russia is the biggest country in the world.
狗是可愛，但貓是最可愛的。
gǒu shì kěài ， dàn māo shì zuì kěài de 。
Dogs are cute, but cats are the cutest.
哪個國家最富有？
nǎ gè guójiā zuì fùyǒu ？
Which country is the richest?
誰說最多黃色笑話？
shuírén shuō zuì duō huángsèxiàohuà ？
Who told the most dirty jokes?

Psychological verb superlatives ##

We can also add 最 (zuì) before verbs related to our psychological states since there is also a degree of how much we feel.
The examples below are of some very common psychological verb superlatives:
我最想你了。
wǒ zuì xiǎng nǐ le 。
I missed you the most!
我最恨你。
wǒ zuì hèn nǐ 。
I hate you the most.
我最欣賞的人是德蕾莎修女。
wǒ zuì xīnshǎng de rén shì délěishā xiūnǚ 。
The person I admire the most is Mother Theresa.
在家裡，我最尊敬我姐姐，但我最妒忌的也是她。
zài jiā lǐ ， wǒ zuì zūnjìng wǒ jiěie ， dàn wǒ zuì dùjì de yě shì tā 。
I respect my sister the most in the family, but I also envy her the most.

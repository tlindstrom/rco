會

HSK Level: 1

會 (huì) is a very common word in Chinese and it can function as a verb or a noun. We use it as a verb to talk about ability or future events while its noun form means meeting, association or moment. Let's see how we can use 會 (huì) as a verb in detail and we will move on to look at the usage of 會 (huì) as a noun.

會 as can or be able to

會 (huì) is used to express whether someone has the ability to do something. It follows the following sentence structure:
subject + 會 + verb + object (optional)
Here are some examples:

你 會 跳舞 嗎 ？
nǐ huì tiàowǔ ma ？
Can you dance?
我 會 煮飯 ， 但 我 不 喜歡 煮 。
wǒ huì zhǔfàn ， dàn wǒ bù xǐhuān zhǔ 。
I can cook but I don't like to cook.
他 很 會 裝 。
hěnhuì hěn huì zhuāng 。
He is very good at pretending.

(As we can see in the above example, when we put degree adverbs such as 很 (hěn) and 太 (tài) before 會 (huì), as in 很會 (hěnhuì) and 太會 (tàihuì), we have a new meaning: to be good at.)

我 會 說 英語 和 俄語 。
wǒ huì shuō yīngyǔ hé éyǔ 。
I can speak English and Russian.
我 會 英語 和 俄語 。
wǒ huì yīngyǔ hé éyǔ 。
I know English and Russian.

(When talking about languages, we can skip the verb after 會 (huì) to mean that we have a general knowledge about a particular language. Just like in the above example, deleting the verb 說 (shuō) in the sentence means that 'I' know English and Russian and 'my' knowledge of the languages is not limited to speaking.)

會 as will

會 (huì) is a very useful word to help us express future events. By simply putting 會 (huì) before the main verb of a sentence, we create a sentence in future tense:
subject + 會 + verb/ verb phrase
For example:

我 會 來 接 你 。
wǒ huì lái jiē nǐ 。
I will pick you up.
我 不 會 撒謊 。
wǒ bù huì sāhuǎng 。
I will not lie.
我 會 愛 你 一生一世 。
wǒ huì ài nǐ yīshēngyīshì 。
I will love you forever.
每個人 都 會 死 。
měigerén dōu huì sǐ 。
Everyone will die.

會 as meeting or conference

會 (huì) as a noun means an activity where people assemble for a purpose. In this sense, the most common translations of 會 (huì) are meeting or conference. Let's have a look at some examples:

我 在 開 會 ， 待會 說 。
wǒ zài kāi huì ， dàihuì shuō 。
I am in a meeting, talk to you later.
我 上 個 星期 去 了 一 個 有關 人權 的 會 。
wǒ shàng ge xīngqī qù le yī ge yǒuguān rénquán de huì 。
I was in conference on human rights last week.
我 昨天 去 了 李 的 追思會 。
wǒ zuótiān qù le lǐ de zhuīsīhuì 。
I went to a memorial meeting of Li yesterday.

會 as association or society

會 (huì) is often used as a noun to describe a group of people who gather together for a particular purpose. To specify what kind of a group it is, we put a noun or verb phrase before 會 (huì):
noun/verb phrase + 會
For example:

我 不 喜歡 我 校 的 學生會 。
wǒ bù xǐhuān wǒ xiào de xuéshēnghuì 。
I don't like the student union of my school.
你 是 委員會 的 委員 嗎 ？
nǐ shì wěiyuánhuì de wěiyuán ma ？
Are you a member of the committee?
我 幾 個 月 前 參加 了 一 個 讀書會 。
wǒ jǐ ge yuè qián cānjiā le yī ge dúshūhuì 。
I joined a book club a few months ago.

會 to express a short period of time

We use 會 (huì) to talk about a short period of time. For example:

等 一 會 ， 我 要 先 把 啤酒 喝 完 。
děng yī huì ， wǒ yào xiān bǎ píjiǔ hē wán 。
Just a moment, I need to finish the beer first.
待 會兒 見 ！
dài huìr jiàn ！
See you later!

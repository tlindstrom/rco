把

把 (bǎ) is a very frequently used word in Chinese, and therefore it is very important for learners to understand its usage. However, due to the lack of an English equivalent, many have found it difficult to put it in a sentence.
In this article, I will discuss each of its possible meanings and their corresponding usage in detail.

把 as a disposal preposition

把 (bǎ) is used to indicate that certain effects have been acted upon an object. Hence, it is also known as the disposal preposition, which does not necessarily mean that an object is being thrown away, but more that an effect is thrown onto an object.
We will first look at how 把 (bǎ), when used as a disposal preposition, will alter the basic sentence structure and see in which situations it is applicable and in which situations it is not.

The 把-structure

To put it simply, the 把-structure changes the basic S-V-O structure into the following structure:
subject + 把 + object + verb
For example, in a non-把 sentence:

我 已經 洗好 所有 床單 了 。
wǒ yǐjīng xǐhǎo suǒyǒu chuángdān le 。
I have already washed all of the bed sheets.

But if it is a 把-structure:

我 已經 把 所有 床單 都 洗好 了 。
wǒ yǐjīng bǎ suǒyǒu chuángdān dōu xǐhǎo le 。
I have already washed all of the bed sheets.

Note that an S-V-O structure is NOT always a good English equivalent for 把-structure sentences. In Chinese, there are cases where the 把-structure sounds more natural and correct than the S-V-O structure. Sentences with the verb 放 (fàng) are a great example:

把-structure with the verb 放 (fàng)

The most common verb used in the 把-structure is 放 (fàng). Using the basic S-V-O structure for this verb will result in unnatural sentences.
Let's look at some examples now:

我 把 手錶 放 在 桌子 上 。
wǒ bǎ shǒubiǎo fàng zài zhuōzǐ shàng 。
I put the watch on the desk.
我 放 手錶 在 桌子 上 。
wǒ fàng shǒubiǎo zài zhuōzǐ shàng 。
我 把 手 放 在 眼 前面 。
wǒ bǎ tóu fàng jìn shuǐ lǐmiàn 。
I put my hand in front of my eyes.
我 放 手 在 眼 前面 。
wǒ fàng shǒu zài yǎn qiánmiàn 。

Some other common examples with 把 (bǎ)

Of course, there are also verbs which are applicable to both the S-V-O structure and the 把-structure. When we want to mention that an action has had a certain effect on an object, we can use the 把-structure as well. as well.
Let's have more examples with other verbs in the 把-structure:

我 把 你的 窗 打破 了 。
wǒ bǎ nǐde chuāng dǎpò le 。
I broke your window.
我 把 床 整理好 好 了 。
wǒ bǎ chuáng zhěnglǐ hǎo le 。
I have made the bed.
你 要 把 我 殺死 嗎 ？
nǐ yào bǎ wǒ shāsǐ ma ？
Do you want to kill me?
我 把 最 好 的 東西 都 給 了 你 。
wǒ bǎ zuìhǎo hǎo de dōngxī dōu gěi le nǐ 。
I have given the best to you.
那 個 嚴厲 的 父親 把 他的 女兒 弄 瘋 了 。
nà ge yánlì de fùqīn bǎ tāde nǚér nòng fēng le 。
The strict father is driven his daughter crazy.

When 把 (bǎ) should not be used

As mentioned in the previous section, 把 (bǎ) is used to indicate that something has been affected, but, furthermore, the result of that effect must be clearly stated as well. For instance, in the example '那個嚴厲的父親把他的女兒弄瘋了', the result that 'the daughter is now crazy' is expressed in the word '瘋 (fēng)', and this is the direct result of her father's action '弄 (nòng)'. If we omit '瘋 (fēng)', the sentence will be ungrammatical because the influence or result is not expressed. Like in the following example, the first sentence is grammatical while the second not because the result '完 (wán)', which means to finish up, is not expressed in the sentence.

我的 哥哥 把 所有 糖 都 用完 了 。
wǒde gēge bǎ suǒyǒu táng dōu yòngwán le 。
My brother has used up all the sugar.
我的 哥哥 把 所有 糖 都 用 了 。
wǒde gēge bǎ suǒyǒu táng dōu yòng le 。

Since result or influence is required by 把 (bǎ) in a sentence, most stative verbs are not compatible with 把 (bǎ) because they do not have any direct effects on their objects. The following are some examples to illustrate this:

我 把 你 想 了 。
wǒ bǎ nǐ xiǎng le 。
我 把 他 姐姐 認識 了 。
wǒ bǎ tā jiěie rènshí le 。
我 把 這 個 見議 不 同意 。
wǒ bǎ ge jiànyì bù tóngyì 。
我 把 我的 數學 老師 討厭 。
wǒ bǎ wǒde shùxué lǎoshī tǎoyàn 。

把 as a classifier

It can also work as a classifier to modify different types of nouns, like in the following:

我 買 了 一 把 扇 。
wǒ mǎi le yī bǎ shàn 。
I have bought a fan.
這 把 椅子 是 我的 。
zhè bǎ yǐzi shì wǒde 。
This chair is mine.
我 爸爸 一 把 年紀 ， 還 喜歡 出去 玩兒 。
wǒ bàba yī bǎ niánjì ， hái xǐhuān chūqù wánr 。
My father is old but still likes going out.
我 要 買 一 把 刀 。
wǒ yào mǎi yī bǎ dāo 。
I am going to buy a knife.

把 as to hold

把 著 我的 手 過 馬路 。
bǎ zhe wǒ de shǒu guò mǎlù 。
Hold my arm and cross the road.
把 著 很 大 酒杯 的 那 個 人 是 我的 姐姐 。
bǎ zhe hěn dà jiǔbēi de nà ge rén shì wǒ de jiějie 。
My sister is the one with the big glass in her hand.
使用 電梯 時 ， 我們 應該 把 住 扶手 。
shǐyòng diàntī shí ， wǒmen bǎ zhù fúshǒu 。
We should hold on to the handrail while taking the escalator.

把 as to guard or to watch

我 負責 把 門 。
wǒ fùzé bǎ mén 。
I was in charge of watching the door.
我 會 把 著 這裡 ， 去 吧 。
wǒ huì bǎ zhe zhèlǐ ， qù ba 。
I will watch here, just go!

把 as handle

這 摩托車 沒 有 車 把 。
zhè zìhángchē méi yǒu chē bǎ 。
The motorbike has no handlebars.
我 家 的 門 把 是 金 造 的 。
wǒ jiā de mén bǎ shì jīn zào de 。
The handle of my door is made of gold.

把 in 把妹 (bǎmèi)

One interesting thing about 把 (bǎ) is that when it goes with 妹 (mèi) as in 把妹(bǎmèi), it means to flirt with girls or to pick up girls. However, it is only used in the colloquial Chinese language and could sound a little bit rude. So think twice if you want to say it to your Chinese teacher!
Knowing that it could be quite an important phrase to some of you, of course we need some examples:

他 又 去 酒吧 把妹 了 ！
tā yòu qù jiǔbā bǎmèi le ！
He went to a bar to pick up girls again!
我 想 他的 正職 是 把妹 吧 。
wǒ xiǎng tā zhèngzhí shì bǎmèi ba 。
I think his main job is picking up girls.

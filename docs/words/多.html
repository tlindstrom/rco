CHINESE WORD

多

A test article about the word 吧

HSK Level: 2

多 (duō) can be used to form questions regarding degree or extent, to emphasize a statement or to express 'much'/many or 'more' in quantity.

多 as more ##

多 (duō) can be used to express to do something more.
多 + verb phrase
我老公要多關注孩子的成長。
wǒ lǎogōng yào duō guānzhù háizi de chéngzhǎng 。
My husband needs to be more concerned about the development of the kids.
多喝牛奶可以幫助我們保持骨骼健康。
duō hē niúnǎi kěyǐ bāngzhù wǒmen bǎochí gǔgé jiànkāng 。
Drinking more milk can keep our bones healthy.
我會多看書。
wǒ huì duō kàn shū 。
I will read more books.
你應該多留意社會發生的事。
duō yīnggāi duō liúyì shèhuì fāshēng de shì 。
You should pay attention to what is happening in society more.

多 to describe great quantity ##

多 (duō) can be used as much, many or a lot to describe that something is in excess or something has been done a lot. For a 'verb' that is done much, we put 多 (duō) after the verb:
verb/verb phrase + 多
For a 'noun' that is much, we put 多 (duō) before the noun:
多 + noun/ noun phrase
他說了很多了。
tā shuō le hěn duō le 。
He has talked a lot already.
你憂慮太多了。
nǐ yōulǜ tài duō le 。
You worry too much.
你為什麼要這麼多錢？
nǐ wèishénme yào zhème duō qián ？
Why do you need that much money?
你落了太多鹽了。
nǐ luò le tài duō yán le 。
You have added too much salt.
你會傷害很多人。
nǐ huì shānghài hěn duō rén 。
You will hurt a lot of people.
Note that if we want to put stress on what is being excessive, we can topicalize it by moving it to the beginning of a sentence and send 太多 (tàiduō) or 很多 (hěnduō) to the end of the sentence. The last two examples in the above can be reconstructed like in the following:
你落的鹽太多了。
nǐ là de yán tài duō le 。
The salt you have added is too much.
你會傷害的人很多。
nǐ huì shānghài de rén hěn duō 。
The people you have hurt are a lot.

多 to ask questions about degree or extent of something ##

多 (duō) can be translated into how in questions regarding degree or extent, such as in how big, how tall and how heavy, etc. To raise questions concerning degree of certain quality, we use 多 (duō) with the following structure:
clause/phrase + 多 + adjective
你能走多遠呢？
nǐ néng zǒu duō yuǎn ne ？
How far can you go?
你會留在這裡多久？
nǐ huì liú zài zhèlǐ duōjiǔ jiǔ ？
How long are you going to stay in here?
你外婆多大了？
nǐ wàipó duō dà le ？
How old is your grandma?
俄羅斯多大呢？
éluósī duō dà ne ？
How big is Russia?
你每個月賺多少呢？
nǐ měi gè yuè zhuàn duōshǎo ne ？
How much do you earn per month?
(Note that to ask 'how much' or 'how many', we use the adjective 少 (shǎo) instead of 多 (duō) even 多 (duō) means much or many. It is possibly because we don't want to have two 多 in a row so 少 (shǎo) has been taken as an alternative.)

多 to emphasize quality ##

When 多 (duō) is put before an adjective, its degree could be intensified. It can be translated into how as in, for example, how great the world is or how beautiful you are. We can put either a verb/noun phrase or a clause before 多 (duō). There are two possible structures we can use to make emphasis with 多 (duō):
1) verb/noun phrase + 多 + adjective
2) clause + , + 多 + adjective
她考試拿滿分，多厲害啊！
tā kǎoshì ná mǎn fēn ， duō lìhài a ！
How brilliant she was to score full marks in the exam!
她每年都會跟我慶祝生日，多好！
tā měi nián dōu huì gēn wǒ qìngzhù shēngrì ， duō hǎo ！
How great she is to celebrate my birthday with me every year!
你多狡猾！
nǐ duō jiǎohuá ！
How cunning you are!
坐商務多昂貴！
zuò shāngwù duō ángguì ！
How expensive it is to fly with business class!

少

HSK Level: 1

少 (shǎo) is used to talk about small/insufficient quantity, it could be about time, money, volume, age, just to name a few. We will discuss the usage of 少 in detail in this article.

少 to express little or few

他 喝 這麼 少 的 水 ， 難怪 他 中暑 了 。
tā hē zhème shǎo de shuǐ ， nánguài tā zhōngshǔ le 。
He didn't drink so much water, no wonder he got a heatstroke.
你 就 賺 這麼 少 ？
nǐ jiù zhuàn zhème shǎo ？
You are earning that little?
醫生 讓 我 少 喝 咖啡 。
yīshēng ràng wǒ shǎo hē kāfēi 。
My doctor told me to drink less coffee.
我們 很 少 見 。
wǒmen hěn shǎo jiàn 。
We don't see each other so often.
你 應該 吃 少一點 。
nǐ yīnggāi chī shǎoyīdiǎn 。
You should eat less.

(Note that when 一點 (yīdiǎn) follows 少 (shǎo), in as 少一點 (shǎoyīdiǎn) , it becomes a comparative form less or fewer.)

她 還 年少 ， 你 不能 期望 她 會 明白 你 。
xiǎo huán niánshào ， nǐ bùnéng qīwàng tā huì míngbái nǐ 。
She is still very young, you can't expect her to understand you.

(In the above example, 年少 literally means 'few years of age'. When 少 is used to describe age, we pronounce it as shào instead of shǎo. Also, keep in mind that 少 cannot stand alone to mean 'young', it must attach to 年 (nián) to produce the meaning 'young'. An alternative of 年少 (shào nián) will be 小 (xiǎo)．Therefore, if we want to say that someone is young, we can use 小 like in this sentence:

孩子 還 小 的 時候 ， 我們 應該 多 陪 他們 。
háizi hái xiǎo de shíhòu ， wǒmen yīnggāi duō péi tāmen 。
We should spend more time with our kids when they are still young.

少 as a verb to describe that something is missed or lacked

When we only have a very small quantity of something, of course we will lack something or something must have been missed. Let's see how we can use 少 to describe them:

他 少 了 一點 信心 。
tā shǎo le yīdiǎn xìnxīn 。
He lacks a little bit of confidence.
我們 還 少 五百 塊 。
wǒmen hái shǎo wǔbǎi kuài 。
We still lack 5000 bucks.
我們 共同進退 ， 一 個 也 不能 少 。
wǒmen gòngtóngjìntuì ， yī gè yě bùnéng shǎo 。
We are all together, no one can be missed.
名單 上 少 了 我的 名字 。
míngdān shàng shǎo le wǒ de míngzì 。
My name is missing on the list.

少 as to stop in imperative sentences

少 (shǎo) is very useful when we want to tell someone to stop doing something. It can be followed by either a noun or verb as in the the structure below:
少 + noun/verb
Some examples here:

少 廢話 ！
shǎo fèihuà ！
Stop the bullshit!
少 吹牛 了 ， 我 知道 你 是 怎樣 的 一 個 人 。
shǎo chuīniú le ， wǒ zhīdào nǐ shì zěnyàng de yī gè rén 。
Stop bragging, I know how you are.
少 說話 ！
shǎo shuōhuà ，
Stop talking!
少 裝 了 ， 我 知道 你 要 什麼 。
shǎo zhuāng le ， wǒ zhīdào nǐ yào shénme 。
Stop pretending, I know what you want.

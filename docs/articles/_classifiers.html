Classifiers, also known as measure words, are used when a noun is quantified by a numeral in the same phrase. We put classifiers between its numeral and noun:
number + classifier + noun

Common Classifiers

Chinese has many classifiers, and when learning, it is best to focus on learning the more frequently used classifiers. The following are some more commonly used classifiers:
那裡有一群學生。
nàlǐ yǒu yī qún xuéshēng 。
There is a group of students.
我買了三包餅乾。
wǒ mǎi le sān bāo bǐnggān 。
I bought three packets of biscuits.
他只是一隻老鼠。
tā zhǐ shì yī zhī lǎoshǔ 。
It is only a mouse.
她建了三所學校。
tā jiàn le sān suǒ xuéxiào 。
She has built three schools.
我找到一條線。
wǒ zhǎo dào yī tiáo xiàn 。
I found one thread.
我要買一支鉛筆。
wǒ yào mǎi yī zhī qiānbǐ 。
I have to buy a pencil.
她喝了一瓶紅酒。
tā hē le yī píng hóngjiǔ 。
She drank a bottle of red wine
我有三千本書。
wǒ yǒu sānqiān běn shū 。
I have three thousand books.
我已經給了他三次機會！
wǒ yǐjīng gěi le tā sān cì jīhuì ！
I gave him three chances already!
Note that the above classifiers are not limited to the nouns given in the examples. It is possible for one classifier to be used for several different types of nouns. Let's take the classifier 塊 (kuài) as an example:
她每天賺五塊錢。
tā měitiān zhuàn wǔ kuài qián 。
She makes five dollars every day.
我還有三塊蛋糕。
wǒ hái yǒu sān kuài dàngāo 。
I still have three pieces of cake.
我在她的家裡找到一塊煤。
wǒ zài tāde jiā lǐ zhǎodào yī kuài méi 。
I found a lump of coal at her home.

個 (ge) ##

個 (ge) is the most common classifier in Chinese. It is used for a huge number of nouns, and one can only learn the usage of 個 (ge) by heart since there is no specific reason for why it is applicable to some nouns but not others.
The following is some examples where 個 (ge) is used as a classifier:
他吃了三個蘋果。
tā chī le sān ge pínguǒ 。
He ate three apples.
她懷孕了兩個月了。
tā huáiyùn le liǎng ge yuè le 。
She has been pregnant for two months.
她有一個問題。
tā yǒu yī ge wèntí 。
She has a question.
我有一個背包。
wǒ yǒu yī ge bèibāo 。
I have a backpack.
我爸爸有三個秘書。
wǒ bàba yǒu sān ge mìshū 。
My father has three secretaries.
When the number is 一 (yī), we don't need to include it:
我有(一)個秘書。
wǒ yǒu yī ge mìshū 。
I have a secretary.
她有(一)個問題。
tā yǒu yī ge wèntí 。
She has a problem.
個 (ge) is so common that sometimes we will use it as a substitute when we can't think of a noun's proper classifier. It won't make you sound unnatural, but be ready for someone to correct you!

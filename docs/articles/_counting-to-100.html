  3. Counting to 100
Counting from one to one hundred is actually very easy in Chinese. First, let's see how to count to ten:
Number Characters Pinyin
0 零 Líng
1 一 Yī
2 二 Èr
3 三 Sān
4 四 Sì
5 五 Wǔ
6 六 Liù
7 七 Qī
8 八 Bā
9 九 Jiǔ
10 十 Shí
我的女兒七歲。
wǒde nǚér qī suì 。
My daughter is seven years old.
這艘船有十個人的空間。
zhè sōu chuán yǒu shí ge rén de kōngjiān 。
There is room for ten people in this boat.
Note that when the number 二 (Èr) is used alone as a single digit number, unless it is used to express the second, we use 兩 (liǎng) instead. Let's have a look at some examples:
我昨天是第二個來到的人。
wǒ zuótiān shì dìèr ge láidào de rén 。
I was the second person who arrived yesterday.
我有兩個孩子。
wǒ yǒu liǎng ge háizǐ 。
I have two children.
我有兩隻狗。
wǒ yǒu liǎng zhī gǒu 。
I have two dogs.
After you have learned how to count to ten, most of the hard work is already done. After ten, you just start counting tens: twenty is 二十 (èr shí) - two tens, thirty is 三十 (sān shí) - three tens, and so on. All the single digits can just be added after those tens, for example, fifty-five is 五十五 (wǔshíwǔ) and sixty-three is 六十三 (liùshísān).

Pretty simple, right? Here are some more examples:
班房裡有三十五個人。
bānfáng lǐ yǒu sānshíwǔ ge rén 。
There are thirty-five people in the class.
我就有五十塊。
wǒ jiù yǒu wǔshí kuài 。
I only have fifty dollars now.
我這個星期工作了六十個小時！
wǒ zhè ge xīngqī gōngzuò le liùshí ge xiǎoshí ！
I worked sixty hours this week!

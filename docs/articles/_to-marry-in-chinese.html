  3. To Marry in Chinese
In Chinese, there are three ways to express to marry.
1) 結婚 (jiéhūn)
2) 娶 (qǔ)
3) 嫁 (jià)
Although they all refer to the concept of marriage in English, they cannot be used interchangeably. They are used in a different sentence structure and have a different implications.

結婚 (jiéhūn) ##

結婚 (jiéhūn) is an intransitive verb which does not take an object. It can only be incorporated into a sentence with the following structure:
subjects + 結婚
Let's look at some examples:
我媽媽跟另外一個男人結婚了。
wǒ māma gēn lìngwài yī ge nánrén jiéhūn le 。
My mother married another man.
我叔叔要和一個日本女人結婚。
wǒ shūshū yào hé yī ge rìběn nǚrén jiéhūn 。
My uncle is going to marry a Japanese woman.
我的祖父母已經結婚了五十年了。
wǒde zǔfùmǔ yǐjīng jiéhūn le wǔshí nián le 。
My grandparents have been married 50 years.

娶 (qǔ) ##

It is a transitive verb which takes an object. The object is restricted to females and the subject must be a male. We should follow the structure when we use the verb 娶 (qǔ):
male subject + 娶 + female object
Let's see how it can be used with some examples:
我娶了世界上最美的女人。
wǒ qǔ le shìjiè shàng zuì měi de nǚrén 。
I have married the most beautiful woman in the world.
在現實中，王子真的會娶灰姑娘嗎？
zài xiànshí zhōng ， wángzǐ zhēnde huì qǔ huīgūniáng ma ？
In the real world, will a prince marry Cinderella?
在古代中國，男人可以娶多於一個女人。
zài gǔdài zhōngguó ， nánrén kěyǐ qǔ duō yú yī ge nǚrén 。
In ancient China, men could marry more than one woman.
If we look at how 娶 is constructed, we will see it is made up of two words: 取 (qǔ) and 女 (nǚ). 取 (qǔ) means to take while 女 (nǚ) means woman. It is easy to understand how 'men marrying women' related to the concept of 'men taking women (home)'. This connotation makes sense because in traditional Chinese culture, women were subordinate to their husbands and were considered to be their husband's private property. Now, while men and women enjoy equal social status, this word is still kept in the language.

嫁 (jià) ##

For this verb, its subject has to be a female and is often followed by the word 給 (gěi) and a male object. It is used in a sentence like this:
female subject + 嫁 + 給 + male object
Examples:
女人不需要嫁給富有的男人，但他一定要正直。
nǚrén bù xūyào jià gěi fùyǒu de nánrén ， dàn tā yīdìng yào zhèngzhí 。
A woman should not necessarily marry a rich man, but she should marry someone upright.
你要嫁給我嗎？
nǐ yào jià gěi wǒ ma ？
Do you want to marry me? (asked by a man)
這個女人被逼嫁給一個狡猾的男人。
nǚ jià nǚrén bèi bī jià gěi yī ge jiǎohuá de nánrén 。
This woman was forced to marry a cunning man.
Again, if we look at the structure of the verb 嫁, we can see how marriage was perceived in ancient China. 嫁 was built from the words 女 (nǚ) and 家 (jiā). Therefore, 'a woman marrying a man' can be interpreted as 'a woman building a family by getting married with a man'. Also, 給 (gěi), which means to give, is used together with the word 嫁 (jià). Therefore, it could imply that 'women getting married' is similar to 'women being given to someone'. In fact, there are still parents who think once their daughter is married, their daughter is given to another family and no longer belongs to their own family.
Here is a well-known Chinese saying about women getting married:
嫁出去的女兒，如潑出去的水。
jià chūqù de nǚér ， rú pō chūqù de shuǐ 。
Married daughters are like water that has been spilled.

Learning how to apologize is definitely one of the first things we want to learn in a new language, and there are many ways to apologize in Chinese. In this article, we will learn the most common three: 對不起 (duìbùqǐ), 不好意思 (bùhǎoyìsi) and 抱歉 (bàoqiàn).

對不起 (duìbùqǐ) ##

We use 對不起 (duìbùqǐ) when we assume responsibility for doing something wrong. The following are some everyday examples:
對不起，我遲到了。
duìbùqǐ ， wǒ chídào le 。
Sorry I'm late.
對不起，我沒有時間。
duìbùqǐ ， wǒ méi yǒu shíjiān 。
Sorry, I have no time.
對不起，我忘了。
duìbùqǐ ， wǒ wàng le 。
Sorry, I forgot all about it.
對不起，我不是這個意思。
duìbùqǐ ， wǒ bù shì zhè gè yìsī 。
Sorry, I didn't mean it that way.

不好意思 (bùhǎoyìsi) ##

不好意思 (bùhǎoyìsi) is almost the same as 對不起 (duìbùqǐ), but it is milder and expresses personal embarrassment for the mistake. If you want to apologize for a more trivial matter, you can use 不好意思 (bùhǎoyìsi) instead. It is the equivalent of excuse me in English.
For example:
不好意思，你可以大聲一點兒嗎？
bùhǎoyìsi ， nǐ kěyǐ dàshēng yīdiǎnér ma ？
Excuse me, can you speak up a bit?
不好意思，我不能來你的生日會。
bùhǎoyìsi ， wǒ bùnéng lái nǐde shēngrì 。
I am sorry, I can't come to your birthday party.
不好意思，我要走了。
bùhǎoyìsi ， wǒ yào zǒu 。
I am sorry but I need to leave now.
不好意思，我可以說幾句嗎？
bùhǎoyìsi ， wǒ shuō jǐ jù ma ？
Excuse me, can I say something?

抱歉 (bàoqiàn) ##

抱歉 (bàoqiàn) is a more formal form of 對不起 (duìbùqǐ), where the speaker assumes public responsibility for a mistake and the inconvenience caused. Recently, it is also sometimes colloquially used to express our regrets for the misfortune that others experience.
對於你媽媽過世的事，我感到很抱歉。
duìyú nǐ māma guòshì de shì ， wǒ gǎndào hěn bàoqiàn 。
I'm really sorry about your mother's death.
很抱歉，店已經關了。
hěn bàoqiàn ， diàn yǐjīng guān le 。
I am really sorry, but the store is already closed.
我聽說你被辭掉了，很抱歉。
wǒ tīngshuō nǐ bèi cídiào le ， hěn bàoqiàn 。
Sorry to hear that you got fired.
抱歉，會議已經取消了。
bàoqiàn ， huìyì yǐjīng qǔxiāo le 。
I am sorry, the meeting has been canceled already.

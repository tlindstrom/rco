讓 as to let ##

讓 (ràng) can be used to talk about granting permission or allowing something to happen, which is very similar to to let in English. Unlike to let in English, 讓 (ràng) can be followed by a clause or none as in the following structure:
Subject + 讓 + clause (optional)

媽媽 不 讓 我 去 。
māma bù ràng wǒ qù 。
Mom won't let me go.
他 想 多 留 一會兒 ， 但 他 父母 不 讓 。
tā xiǎng duō liú yīhuìr ， dàn tā fùmǔ bù ràng 。
He wanted to stay a bit longer but his parents wouldn't let him.
讓 我 走 ！
ràng wǒ zǒu ！
Let me go!
讓 它 順其自然 吧 。
ràng tā shùnqízìrán ba 。
Just let it happen naturally.

讓 as to make ##

讓 (ràng) can function as a causative verb, which indicates that someone/something causes something to happen. It can be translated to to make in English and must be followed by a clause as in the structure below:
subject + 讓 + clause
For example:

他的 故事 讓 我們 很 驚喜 。
tā de gùshi ràng wǒmen hěn jīngxǐ 。
His story made us very surprised.
這 個 味道 讓 我 想起 我 外婆 。
zhè gè wèidào ràng wǒ xiǎngqǐ wǒ wàipó 。
This smell made me think of my grandma.
看 着 你的 笑容 ， 讓 我 也 笑 了 。
kàn zhe nǐ de xiàoróng ， ràng wǒ yě xiào le 。
Looking at your smile makes me smile as well.
這 首 歌 讓 我 很 感動 。
zhè shǒu gē ràng wǒ hěn gǎndòng 。
The song makes me touched.

讓 as to give up or to offer ##

We can use 讓 (ràng) as a verb to express giving up or offering something that we own to someone else. 讓 (ràng) is a ditransitive verb that must be followed by an direct object and indirect object.
subject + 讓 + direct object + 給 + indirect object
(The direct object can only be omitted when it is known to the hearer already)

他 讓 了 他的 職位 給 他的 老婆 。
tā ràng le tā de zhíwèi gěi tā de lǎopó 。
He gave up his position to his wife.
我 爸爸 把 整 個 公司 讓 了 給 我 。
wǒ bàba bǎ zhěng gè gōngsī ràng le gěi wǒ 。
My father gave up his whole company to me.
我 讓 了 我的 座位 給 那 個 老人家 。
wǒ ràng le wǒ de zuòwèi gěi nà gè lǎorénjiā 。
I offered my seat to the elderly.

If the direct object in the above example is known to the hearer already, we can also form a sentence like this without changing its meaning:

我 讓 了 給 那 個 老人家 。
wǒ ràng le gěi nà gè lǎorénjiā 。
I offered (my seat) to the elderly.

讓 as to give way ##

We can use 讓 (ràng) to mean to give way when we want to let other people or vehicles to go before us or to stop arguing with someone or fighting against someone for something. Let's look at some examples:

雖然 他 是 我的 弟弟 ， 但 不 代表 我 要 經常 讓 他 。
suīrán tā shì wǒ de dìdi ， dàn bù dàibiǎo wǒ yào jīngcháng ràng tā 。
He is my brother but it doesn't mean I have to give way all the time.
三 個 小時 過 了 ， 他們 還是 不 讓 對方 。
sān gè xiǎoshí guò le ， tāmen háishì bù ràng duìfāng 。
After three hours, they still don't want to give way to each other.
他 誰 都 不 讓 ， 一定 要 贏 。
tā shuí dōu bù ràng ， yīdìng yào yíng 。
He has to win this time and no one can stand on his way for it.
不好意思 ， 可以 讓 讓 嗎 ？
bùhǎoyìsi ， kěyǐ ràng ràng ma ？
Excuse me, can I go past?

讓 as a passive preposition ##

讓 (ràng) can be used to construct a passive voice sentence. It is very similar to 被 but is more colloquial. Unlike 被 , the action doer must be included in a 讓-passive sentence as in the following structure:
action recipient + 讓 + action doer + verb

錢 讓 人 拿 走 了 。
qián ràng rén ná zǒu le 。
The money got taken away by someone.
房子 讓 大 火 燒 了 。
fángzǐ ràng dà huǒ shāo le 。
The house was burned down by the huge fire.
蛋糕 讓 哥哥 吃 掉 了 。
dàngāo ràng gēge chī diào le 。
The cake got eaten by my brother.
老師 讓 學生 取笑 了 。
lǎoshī ràng xuéshēng qǔxiào le 。
The teacher was teased by his students.

讓 as to tell (suggestions or advice)##

We can use 讓 (ràng) as a verb to tell someone to do something when we give suggestions or commands.

醫生 讓 我 多 休息 。
yīshēng ràng wǒ duō xiūxi 。
The doctor told me to rest more.
媽媽 讓 我 努力 讀書 。
māma ràng wǒ nǔlì dúshū 。
My mum told me to study hard.
我 同學 讓 我 在 課堂 裡 閉嘴 。
wǒ tóngxué ràng wǒ zài kètáng lǐ bìzuǐ 。
My classmates told me to shut up in class.
我 隊友 讓 我 繼續 下去 。
wǒ duìyǒu ràng wǒ jìxù xiàqù 。
My teammates told me to keep it up.

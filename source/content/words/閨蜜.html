閨蜜 (guīmì) is used to describe a female close friend. This is a newly coined word that originated in the internet few years ago. 閨蜜 (guīmì) can be understood as 閨中密友 (guīzhōng mìyǒu). 閨 (guī) means a girl's bedroom in ancient China; 密友 (mìyǒu) means close friends in which 密 (mì) is a homophone of 蜜 (mì) that means honey. Therefore, a 閨蜜 (guīmì) is a very close female friend.
Here are some examples with 閨蜜 (guīmì):
一個閨蜜就夠了。
yī gè guīmì jiù gòu le 。
One (female) close friend is enough.
讓我介紹我的閨蜜給你認識。
ràng wǒ jièshào wǒ de guīmì gěi nǐ rènshí 。
Let me introduce my (female) close friends to you.
閨蜜 (guīmì) originally just describes a female friend but as it has become a more popular term, we can nowadays use it to describe a male friend as well by adding 男 (nán) in the beginning, as in 男閨蜜 (nán guīmì). The following is some examples:
你要男閨蜜還是女閨蜜？
nǐ yào nán guīmì háishì nǚ guīmì ？
What do you prefer, male close friends or female close friends?
(In the above example, we add 女 (nǚ) before 閨蜜 (guīmì) to refer to female close friends because we want to put emphasis on the gender. By default, 閨蜜 (guīmì) means 'female close friend' already.)
她說他是她的「男閨蜜」，我才不信！
tā shuō tā shì tā de 「 nánguīmì 」 ， wǒ cái bù xìn ！
She said he is her 'male close friend', I don't believe it!

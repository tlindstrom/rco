說 (shuō) is a very versatile and useful word. We use this word to refer to a lot of actions that involve moving our mouth to produce a speech, which explains why 言 (yán) , which means speech or word, is the radical of 說 (shuō). The most common translations of 說 (shuō) are to say, to speak, to talk and to tell.

說 as to say##

你跟她說了什麼？
nǐ gēn tā shuō le shénme ？
What did you tell her?
我說你是垃圾。
wǒ shuō nǐ shì lājī 。
I said you were a rubbish.
我說說而已，沒什麼大不了。
wǒ shuōshuō éryǐ ， méishénme dàbùliǎo 。
I am just saying, no big deal.

說 as to speak##

我能說十種語言。
wǒ néng shuō shí zhǒng yǔyán 。
I can speak ten languages.
你為什麼不跟我說話？
nǐ wèishénme bù gēn wǒ shuōhuà ？
Why don't you speak with me?
(When we refer to having a conversation with someone without a specific thing to 'speak' like in the above example, we use 說話 (shuōhuà) instead of 說 (shuō) alone.)
你可以說大聲一點嗎？
nǐ kěyǐ shuō dàshēng yīdiǎn ma ？
Can you speak louder please?

說 as to talk##

別再說了，好嗎？
bié zài shuō le ， hǎoma ？
Stop talking, OK?
你說了很久了，你不累嗎？
nǐ shuō le hěn jiǔ le ， nǐ bù léi ma ？
You have been talking for a long time, don't you feel tired?
她現在能說話了。
tā xiànzài néng shuōhuà le 。
She can talk now.
(When we refer to having the ability to produce speech, we use 說話 (shuōhuà) instead of 說 (shuō) alone.)

說 as to tell##

你會說實話，對嗎？
nǐ huì shuō shíhuà ， duìma ？
You will tell the truth, right?
你說謊！
nǐ shuō huǎng ！
You are lying!
我說不出這兩杯茶的分別。
wǒ shuō bù chū zhè liǎng bēi chá de fēnbié 。
I can't tell the difference between these two cups of tea.
Note that when 說 (shuō) means to tell, it can only be followed by a direct object but not an indirect object. For example, I told her already, her is an indirect object , since 說 (shuō) cannot take up an indirect object right after, we use another word 告訴 (gàosù) instead. Therefore, I told her already is translated into the following:
我已經告訴了她。
wǒ yǐjīng gàosù le tā 。
I told her already.
But not:
我已經說了她。
wǒ yǐjīng shuō le tā 。

There are so many ways to form a question in Chinese, adding 嗎 (ma) at the end of a sentence is one of the most common ways to do so. 嗎 (ma) is used for two types of questions: yes-no questions and tag questions, which are different from those questions formed by another common question marker 呢 (le) . In this article, we will focus on learning to use 嗎 (ma) to form questions.

To ask yes-no questions

By simply putting 嗎 (ma) at the end of a statement, we can form a yes-no question. So easy it is! Let's go through some examples now:

這 個 世界 有 鬼 嗎 ？
zhè gè shìjiè yǒu guǐ ma ？
Are there ghosts in the world?
普通話 發音 容易 嗎 ？
pǔtōnghuà fāyīn róngyì ma ？
Is Mandarin pronunciation easy?
你 洗澡 了 嗎 ？
nǐ xǐzǎo le ma ？
Have you taken shower?
你 會 離開 我 嗎 ？
nǐ huì líkāi wǒ ma ？
Will you leave me?

To ask tag questions

We can use 嗎 (ma) to form a tag question as well. Some common question tags are: 對嗎， 好嗎, 行嗎 and 是嗎. A tag question is used to look for confirmation of your statement so we can simply add question tags after statements which we are not sure about.
Let's have a look at some simple examples:

你 不 吃 魚 ， 對 嗎 ？
nǐ bù chī yú ， duì ma ？
You don't eat fish, right?
你 來 幫 我 ， 好 嗎 ？
nǐ lái bāng wǒ ， hǎo ma ？
You will come to help me, ok?
我們 去 義大利 餐廳 ， 行 嗎 ？
wǒmen qù yìdàlì cāntīng ， xíng ma ？
We are going to an Italian restaurant, ok?
你 胖 了 ， 是 嗎 ？
nǐ pàn le ， shì ma ？
You have become fatter, right?

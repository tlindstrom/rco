嗎 is a grammar particle used to form questions.

Asking yes/no questions

statement + 嗎

By simply putting 嗎 at the end of a statement, we can form a yes-no question. For example, take the following statement:  你 喜歡 喝 咖啡 。 which means "You like to drink coffee." Now, add 嗎 to the end of it:  你 喜歡 喝 咖啡 嗎 ？ which means "Do you like to drink coffee?" Note that when turning the statement into a question, the only difference is 嗎. There are no other changes to the words or their order.

這 個 世界 有 鬼 嗎
Zhè ge shìjiè yǒu guǐ ma?
Do ghosts exist?
普通話 發音 容易 嗎 ？
Pǔtōnghuà fāyīn róngyì ma?
Is Mandarin pronunciation easy?
你 洗澡 了 嗎 ？
Nǐ xǐzǎo le ma ？
Have you taken shower?
你 會 離開 我 嗎 ？
Nǐ huì líkāi wǒ ma?
Are you going to leave me?

Tag questions

statement + tag + 嗎

A tag question is used when you want confirmation of something you said. You simply add question tags after statements that you aren't sure about. Some common question tags are:  對嗎 , 好嗎 , 行嗎 , and 是嗎 .

你 不 吃 魚 ， 對 嗎 ？
Nǐ bù chī yú, duì ma?
You don't eat fish, right?
我 明天 去 找 你 ， 好 嗎 ？
Wǒ míngtiān qù zhǎo nǐ, hǎo ma?
I'll meet up with you tomorrow, ok?
我們 去 義大利 餐廳 ， 行 嗎 ？
Wǒmen qù yìdàlì cāntīng, xíng ma?
Let's go to an Italian restaurant, ok?
你 胖 了 ， 是 嗎 ？
Nǐ pàng le, shì ma?
You've gotten fat, haven't you?

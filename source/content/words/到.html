到 (dào) can serve as a content word as well as a function word. When it is a content word, it has the following possible meanings:
1) to go to
2) to arrive
When it is a function word, it can be used as follows:
1) result complement
2) an equivalent of until or to
We will go through each of them in detail. Let's first have a look at the usage of 到 (dào) as a function word.

到 (dào) as to go to

到 + destination

我 明天 到 教堂 祈禱 。
wǒ míngtiān dào jiàotáng qídǎo 。
I am going to the Church to pray tomorrow.
你 到 印度 幹嘛 ？
nǐ dào yìndù gàn ？
What are you doing in India?
到 英國 讀書 ， 要 用 很 多 錢 。
dào yīngguó dúshū ， yào yòng hěn duō qián 。
It costs a lot to go to the UK to study.
我 要 到 學校 見 校長 。
wǒ yào dào xuéxiào jiàn xiàocháng 。
I need to go to the school to see the principal.

到 (dào) as to arrive

It could be followed by either nothing or a noun (destination). When it is followed by a noun, 到 (dào) means to arrive at.
So it could have the following structure:
到 + destination (optional)

你 什麼 時候 到 ？
nǐ shénme shíhòu dào ？
When are you arriving?
你 到 了 沒有 ？
nǐ dào le méiyǒu ？
Have you arrived yet?
到 了 意大利 後 ， 打 給 我 。
dào le yìdàlì hòu ， dǎ gěi wǒ 。
Give me a call after you have arrived at Italy.
敵人 一 到 了 這 條 村 後 ， 便 展開 大屠殺 。
dírén yī dào le zhè tiáo cūn hòu ， biàn zhǎnkāi dàtúshā 。
The enemies started the massacre once they arrived at this village.

The usage of 到 (dào)is quite straight forward when it functions as a content word because there are English equivalents. Now we will move on to the rather tricky function of 到 (dào) as a result complement.

到 (dào) as a result complement

Result complements are complements that describe the result of a verb and 到 (dào) is one of the most commonly used ones. It could be used with a lot of verbs, such as 聽 (tīng). The action 聽 (tīng) just describes the process of hearing but it does not indicate whether the hearer is able to hear something or not. If we add the result complement 到 (dào) after 聽 (tīng), as in 聽到 (tīngdào), it means the hearer is able to hear something. In other words, the desired result of the action is achieved. If we want to further elaborate what exactly the result is, we can simply put the result after 到 (dào), as in the following structure:
verb + 到 + desire result (optional) It is very important to understand the concept of result complements in Chinese because they are a special, frequently used element of the language that you cannot find in English.
In the following, I will include examples of the same verbs both with and without 到 (dào) to show how this complement can bring the additional information of 'obtaining a result' to its verb.

你 可以 去 看看 她 是 不 是 在 睡覺 嗎 ？
nǐ kěyǐ qù kànkan tā shì bù shì zài shuìjiào ma ？
Can you go to see whether she is sleeping?
我 看 到 她 在 玩 電腦 。
wǒ kàn dào tā zài wán diànnǎo 。
I saw her playing computer games.

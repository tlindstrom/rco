哪裡 (nǎlǐ) is used as where to ask questions about locations, to mean anywhere, everywhere or wherever, or to function as a humble response after a compliment.

哪裡 as where in questions

We use 哪裡 (nǎlǐ) to ask where something is. For example:

我的 書 在 哪裡 ？
wǒ de shū zài nǎlǐ ？
Where is my book?
你 在 哪裡 長大 ？
nǐ zài nǎlǐ zhǎngdà ？
Where did you grow up in?
你 哪裡 覺得 痛 ？
nǐ nǎlǐ juéde tòng ？
Where do you feel the pain?

哪裡 as wherever, anywhere or everywhere

哪裡 (nǎlǐ) is used when there is no specific location referent of 'where'. The common translations used are wherever, anywhere or everywhere. 都 (dōu) usually follows 哪裡 (nǎlǐ) in this kind of expressions.
Here are some examples:

我 去 到 哪裡 都 要 吃 。
wǒ qù dào nǎlǐ dōu yào chī 。
I want to eat wherever I go to.
你 去 哪裡 ， 我 就 去 哪裡 。
nǐ qù nǎlǐ ， wǒ jiù qù nǎlǐ 。
I will go to wherever you go.
我 哪裡 都 不 想 去 。
wǒ nǎlǐ dōu bù xiǎng qù 。
I don't want to go to anywhere.
這裡 哪裡 都 是 人 。
zhèlǐ nǎlǐ dōu shì rén 。
Here are people everywhere.

哪裡 as a humble response after receiving a compliment

哪裡哪裡 (nǎlǐ nǎlǐ) is often used as a humble response after we have received a compliment. Its literal translation is 'where where' and this expression has no English equivalents. In the following, I will use 'where where' as the translation of 哪裡哪裡 (nǎlǐ nǎlǐ):

哪裡哪裡 ， 我 只是 幸運 而已 。
nǎlǐnǎlǐ ， wǒ zhīshì xìngyùn éryǐ 。
'Where where', I was just lucky.
哪裡哪裡 ， 這 是 應該 的 。
nǎlǐnǎlǐ ， zhè shì yīnggāi de 。
'Where where', it was what I should do.
哪裡哪裡 ， 比 我們 好 的 大有人在 。
nǎlǐnǎlǐ ， bǐ wǒmen hǎo de dà 。
'Where where', there are many more people who are better than us.

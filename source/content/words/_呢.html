We can use 呢 (ne) to either form a question, indicate that an action is in an on-going state or emphasize a statement.
We use 呢 (ne) a lot to form a question. Many will link this particle to 嗎 (ma) because both are very commonly used for forming questions. I understand it could be quite tempting to mix them up but DON'T! 呢 (ne) is used to ask follow-up questions, wh-questions and affirmative-negative questions while 嗎 (ma) is used to ask a different kind of question. For further details of the usage of 嗎 (ma), check this out: To Ask Questions with Ma.
Let's have a look at how 呢 (ne) can be used to form different questions, we would move on to talk about how it is used as a continuous aspect marker and an emphatic particle afterwards.

To ask follow-up questions as in what about or how about

呢 (ne) is used to follow up what has been said or to bounce back a question, which is very similar to what about or how about in English.
It is very simple to use and I guess you might find the following exchanges familiar already:

我 姐姐 能 跑 得 很 快 。
wǒ jiějie néng pǎo de hěn kuài 。
My sister can run very fast.
你 呢 ？
nǐ ne ？
What about you?

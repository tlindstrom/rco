的 is a very common particle, used primarily to attach descriptions to what they’re describing.

Basic descriptions

Let’s take a look at a 男人 “man.” We know nothing about him - what he looks like, acts like, and so forth . We can add 的 to the end of some descriptions and put them before 男人. 很 高 的 男人 "very tall man." 善良 的 男人 "kind man."

This is the fundamental function of 的 - connecting descriptions to things. Throughout the website it, you’ll see it called the “(adjectivizing particle),” because that’s precisely what it does - turns words or phrases into adjectives and links them to a head noun.

[adj. + 的]
白色 的 沙
báisè de shā
white sand
最 長 的 河流
zuì cháng de héliú
the longest river
她 是 一 個 很 快樂 的 女孩 。
Tā shì yī gè hěn kuàilè de nǚhái.
She is a very happy girl.

Showing ownership

Nouns can be descriptions, too. Let’s take 那 個 男人 “that man” from before, but we’ll add 那 個 男人 的 吉他 “that man’s guitar.” This time, the guitar is the main subject of focus, while 男人 的 "man’s" is a description of the guitar and shows the guitar belongs to him.

Essentially, when placed after a noun, 的 acts like a possessive “ ‘s ” in English.

[noun + 的 ]

媽媽 的 衣服
Māma de yīfú
Mom's clothes
那 個 男孩 的 狗
Nà ge nánhái de gǒu
That boy's dog
蘇珊 的 頭髮 很 長
sūshān de tóufà hěn cháng.
Susan's hair is very long.

The same goes for pronouns, as well, like 我 "I" becoming 我的 "my," 她 she" becoming 她的 "her," 他媽 "his mom" becoming 他媽的 "his mother's," etc.

[pronoun + 的 ]

我 的 書 。
wǒ de shū.
my book
你 的 鼻子 。
nǐ de bízi
your nose
她 的 手機 壞掉 了 。
Tā de shǒujī huàidiào le.
Her mobile phone broke.

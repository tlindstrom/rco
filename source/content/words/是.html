是 (shì) should definitely be one of the first words to be learnt because it is so frequently used that it is almost impossible not to see 是 (shì) in any writings. Many translate 是 (shì) into to be in English but 是 (shì) is much more versatile that serves more than just to be. In this article, we will go through them one by one with their usage in detail.

是 as to be ##

In general, 是 (shì) is used to introduce a predicative which is used to give further explanation or description of a topic. Unlike to be in English, 是 (shì) can only be followed by a noun/pronoun phrase. Therefore, 是 (shì) can function as the verb to be as in she is a girl but not she is beautiful or she is there. In other words, 是 (shì) is used to connect two nouns/pronouns as in the following:
noun/pronouns + 是 + noun/pronouns
小籠包是有名的上海菜。
xiǎolóngbāo shì zhùmíng de shànghǎi cài 。
Xiaolongbao is a famous Shanghainese dish.
他是在歐洲長大的華人。
tā shì zài ōuzhōu zhǎngdà de huárén 。
He is a Chinese who grew up in Europe.
那個開門的女人是我的姐姐。
nà gè kāi mén de nǚrén shì wǒ de jiějie 。
The woman who opened the door is my sister.
你在使用的杯是我老師送給我的禮物。
nǐ zài shǐyòng de bēi shì wǒ lǎoshī sòng gěi wǒ de lǐwù 。
The cup you are using is a gift from my teacher.
最矮的人是你。
zuì ǎi de rén shì nǐ 。
The shortest person is you.
Note that if we wantdescribe a noun with an adjective, we should avoid using 是 (shì). Instead, we should use the intensifying adverb 很 (hěn) to connect the noun and the adjective. For example:
我的手是乾。
wǒ de shǒu shì gān 。
我的手很乾。
wǒ de shǒu hěn qián
My hand is very dry.
In fact, the left side and the right side of 是 (shì) can be replaced by a 的-expression. It could be an expression with 的 (de) attached to a pronoun, adjective, verb or clause. In this case, 的 (de), which usually functions as an adjectivizing particle, has no specific function or meaning but just a grammar particle required by 是 (shì). The examples below are sentences with 的-expressions on the right side of 是 (shì):
noun + 是 + pronoun-的
這件外套是我的！
zhè jiàn wàitào shì wǒ de ！
This jacket is mine!
這個地方是她的。
zhè gè dìfāng shì tā de 。
This place is hers.
這枝鉛筆是他的。
zhè zhī qiānbǐ shì tā de 。
The pencil is his.
noun + 是 + adjective-的
孩子是無辜的。
háizi shì wúgū de 。
Children are innocent.
水是透明的。
shuǐ shì tòumíng de 。
Water is transparent.
人類是複雜的。
rénlèi shì fùzá de 。
Humans are complicated.
noun + 是 + verb-的
我媽是去的。
wǒ mā shì qù de 。
My mum is indeed going.
老師是教育人們的。
lǎoshī shì jiàoyù rénmén de 。
Teacher is for educating people.
我是會哭的。
wǒ shì huì kū de 。
I will cry.
(Note that in the above example, it expresses that 'I' will cry as a normal human being but not to express that I will cry at a certain time or in a certain occasion in the future.) noun + 是 + clause-的
這道菜是我煮的。
zhè dào cài shì wǒ zhǔ de 。
It was me who cooked the dish.
真相是我說的。
zhēnxiāng shì wǒ shuō de 。
It was me who told the truth.
規則是人定的。
guīzé shì rén dìng de
It was the people who created the rule.
Of course, the left side can be swapped with the right hand side of 是 (shì), so the 的-expressions in the above examples can be placed in the beginning of a sentence as well. Note that such a change could cause in a shift of focus and usually the element in the beginning of sentence is often emphasized. Let's have a look at how some of the examples in the above can be re-formatted in the following:
他的是這枝鉛筆。
tā de shì zhè zhī qiānbǐ 。
What is his is this pencil.
複雜的是人類。
fùzá de shì rénlèi 。
What are complicated are humans.
去的是我媽。
qù de shì wǒ mā 。
The one who is going is my mum.
我煮的是這道菜。
wǒ zhǔ de shì zhè dào cài 。
What I cooked was this dish.
Can both sides of 是 (shì) have 的-expressions? Yes, we can. The examples below are totally acceptable and correct:
你的是我的。
nǐ de shì wǒ de 。
Yours is mine.
他說的是我不想聽的。
tā shuō de shì wǒ bù xiǎng tīng de 。
What he said was what I didn't want to hear.
我做的是錯的。
wǒ zuò de shì cuò de 。
What I did was wrong.
走的都是我討厭的。
zǒu de dōu shì wǒ tǎoyàn de 。
All the people who have gone were people I hate.

是 used in existential sentences ##

是 (shì) can be used to indicate the existence of something, which is similar to another existential marker 有 (yǒu). 是 (shì) indicates the existence of a sole object in a place while 有 (yǒu) indicate the existence of one of the entities that are in the same place. For more information about the usage of 有 (yǒu), click here. We can use 是 (shì) to connect a locational phrase with an entity that exists in that location:
location + 是 + existing entity
我袋子裡是一隻猫。
wǒ dàizǐ lǐ shì yī zhī māo 。
What is in my bag is a cat.
桌子上是我的書本。
zhuōzǐ shàng shì wǒ de shūběn 。
What is on the desk is my book.
醫院前面是學校。
yīyuàn duìmiàn shì xuéxiào 。
What is in front of the hospital is a school.
床上都是水。
chuáng shàng dōu shì shuǐ 。
What is all over my bed is water.

是 used to make emphasis ##

是 (shì) could be very useful when we want to put stress on a sentence. Unlike the '是...的-construction' which emphasizes a certain detail of a piece of information, 是 (shì) is used to emphasize the whole sentence and is put after the subject of a sentence:
subject + 是
她是偷了你的錢，但不代表她是壞人。
tā shì tōu le nǐ de qián ， dàn bù dàibiǎo tā shì huài rén 。
She did steal your money but it doesn't mean she is a bad person.
我是喜歡這個袋子，但它太貴了。
wǒ shì xǐhuān zhè gè dàizǐ ， dàn tā tài guì le 。
I did like the bag but it is too expensive.
我是撒了謊，又怎樣？
wǒ shì sā le huǎng ， yòuzěnyàng ？
I did lie, so what?
對，我是在逃避她。
duì ， wǒ shì zài táobì tā 。
You are right, I am avoiding her.

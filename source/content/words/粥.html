粥 (zhōu) refers to congee, which is a kind of rice porridge that is mostly popular among Asian countries. In China, congee often comes with meats, preserved eggs or pickled vegetables. Congee without any side ingredients are called 白粥 (báizhōu), which means plain congee.
Let's look at how we can use 粥 (zhōu) in a sentence:

我 可以 早餐 吃 粥 嗎 ？
wǒ kěyǐ zǎocān chī zhōu ma ？
Can I have congee for breakfast?
我 不 喜歡 吃 麵 ， 我 喜歡 吃 粥 。
wǒ bù xǐhuān chī miàn ， wǒ xǐhuān chī zhōu 。
I don't like noodles, I like congee.

There is a popular saying '僧多粥少 (sēngduō zhōushǎo)', which can be literally translated into many monks and little congee. We use this to describe when there is a higher demand than supply or when something is very demanding. For example:

這 類型 的 工作 多 是 僧多粥少 ， 被 拒絕 也 不 奇 。
zhè lèixíng de gōngzuò duō shì sēngduō zhōushǎo ， bèi jùjué yě bù qí 。
It is always very competitive to get this kind of job, it is not surprising to get rejected.
香港 樓房 僧多粥少 ， 我 什麼 時候 才 能 買 一 間 呢 ？
xiānggǎng lóufáng sēngduō zhōushǎo ， wǒ shénme shíhòu cái néng mǎi yī jiān ne ？
The houses in Hong Hong are very demanding, when can I buy one myself?

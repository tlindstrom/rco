那裡 (nàlǐ) means there.
那裡 的 食物 很 棒 。
nàlǐ de shíwù hěn bàng 。
The food is great there.
那裡 是 我的 家鄉 。
nàlǐ shì wǒ de jiāxiāng 。
It's my hometown there.
我們 搬 去 海邊 那裡 吧 。
wǒmen bān qù hǎibiān nàlǐ ba 。
Let's move to the coastal (there).
In the last example, 那裡 (nàlǐ) is not necessary. 我們搬去海邊吧 (wǒmen bān qù hǎibiān ba) is still grammatical and share almost the same meaning with 我們搬去海邊那裡吧 (wǒmen bān qù hǎibiān nàlǐ ba). With 那裡 (nàlǐ), the place 'over there but not here' is emphasized. However, the difference is so trivial that we can consider 我們搬去海邊吧 (wǒmen bān qù hǎibiān ba) and 我們搬去海邊那裡吧 (wǒmen bān qù hǎibiān nàlǐ ba) the same. We can also say 我們搬去那裡吧 (wǒmen bān qù nàlǐ ba) if the hearer knows where 'there' is. Therefore we can say this as well:
我們 搬 去 那裡 吧 。
wǒmen bān qù nàlǐ ba 。
Let's move there.

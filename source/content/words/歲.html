We use 歲 (suì) to express our age. We can translate it into years old as in 'I am 5 years old'. 歲 (suì) is used in the following structure:
subject + number + 歲
For example:
我十歲。
wǒ shí suì 。
I am 10 years old.
他已經三十歲了，還不懂自己照顧自己。
tā yǐjīng sānshí suì le ， hái bùdǒng zìjǐ zhàogù zìjǐ 。
He is already 30 years old but still can't take care of himself.
我快二十五歲了。
wǒ kuài èrshíwǔ suì le 。
I am turning 25 years old soon.
(Note that we don't need to add 是 (shì), which means to be, between the subject and the number.)
When we ask how old someone is, we do not use the expression '你多老 (nǐduōlǎo)', which is the Chinese equivalent of 'how old are you', because it gives an implication that 'you' are old; instead, we use some other expressions, such as 你多少歲 (nǐduōshǎosuì), which literally means 'how many years of age you have', to ask for someone's age. In this sense, 歲 (suì) is translated into year of age. Let's have a look at some examples below:
外公多少歲？
wàigōng duōshǎo suì ？
How old is grandfather?
你看起來頗年輕，你多少歲了？
nǐ kànqǐlái pō niánqīng ， nǐ duōshǎo suì le ？
You look quite young, how old are you?
多少歲可以吸煙？
duōshǎo suì kěyǐ xīyān ？
At what age can people smoke?

Chapter 1 - Welcome to RCO

Hello! Welcome to Read Chinese Online.

This is the first chapter of our Introduction to the Chinese Language. This course starts from the very beginning and teaches you the fundamentals of Mandarin Chinese grammar and vocabulary. It will also teach you how to use Read Chinese Online as a learning tool.

Let's get started!

How to Use RCO

The Chinese word for I is 我. Notice how you can hover your mouse pointer over it to show its meaning,and also its pronunciation. If you left-click words, it will take you to the the word's word page, which contains detailed information and example sentences. You can also right-click it for a dropdown-menu where you can choose to listen to its pronunciation.

Now, let's check out our first Chinese sentence:

我 喜歡 你 。
wǒ xǐhuān nǐ.
I like you.

Try right-clicking the sentence above. Notice how you can choose to listen to just one word, or the entire sentence. Chinese pronunciation isn't easy, so it's a good idea to listen both to how the separate words, and the full sentence, are pronounced.

Traditional and Simplified Chinese

As you might be aware, written Chinese comes in two variants, Simplified Chinese and Traditional Chinese. Simplified is used in mainland China and Singapore, while Hong Kong and Taiwan use the traditional characters.

Good news though - no matter which is your preferred script, we've got you covered! Read Chinese Online can be viewed using either traditional or simplified characters. At the top right corner of the page you can select which script you want to use. And you flip it back and forth, if you're curious as to how a particular page will look different in traditional and simplified!

Parallel Texts

In addition to the regular articles, Read Chinese Online also has a collection of parallel texts. These pages have a full Chinese text on the left side of the page, and its English translation on the right side. When you hover your mouse point over a sentence, you'll notice how it lights up on both sides, so you don't lose track of where you are in the text. And just like on any other page, you can click the individual words to go to their word pages, or right click to listen to the pronunciation of words and sentences.

Parallel texts give you an awesome chance to put your Chinese reading skills to the test, while allowing you to "cheat" by looking up words quickly. And remember, we have texts at all difficulty levels!

Feel free to explore our archive of parallel texts!

That sums up the core functionality of Read Chinese Online. Welcome! Now, if you're ready to learn some Chinese, in the next chapter we'll start digging into some Chinese grammar and vocabulary.

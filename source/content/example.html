Interactive Chinese Text

Here's an example of a Chinese word inside a text block: 我們. It needs three attributes: data-word to mark it as a word, and data-meaning and data-pinyin for the English translation and the pinyin.

You can also create a Chinese sentence inside of a paragraph, or anywhere else, for that matter. To keep it clean, it's a good idea to format the sentence nicely, with indentation, like this: 你 想 喝 什麼 ？ Notice how the words in a sentence are exactly like the standalone words. You just wrap it with the sentence tags to turn it into a sentence. Adding the sentnece tags makes it light up like a sentence, and allows users to choose "listen to sentence" from the context menu.

There's a particular formatting for example sentences, which looks like this:

我 不 常 去 圖書館 。
wǒ bù cháng qù túshūguǎn 。
I don't go to the library often.

It consists of a Chinese sentence, followed by lines describing pinyin and meaning, and everything being wrapped in an .example-sentence tag.

The parallel texts look like this:

今天 是 星期天 ， 張一 和 張二 不 用 工作 。 這 是 他們 在 咖啡廳 裡 的 對話 ： Today is Sunday, Zhangyi and Zhanger don’t have to work. This is their conversation in a coffee shop:
張一 ： 明天 又 要 上班 了 ！ Zhangyi: Tomorrow I have to work again!

As you can see, the parallel text is simply an HTML table marked with the ptext class. Each row in the table corresponds to a paragraph in the text. The table has two columns: The left column contains the Chinese sentences (using the exact same format as Chinese sentences anywhere else on the site), and the right column contains the corresponding English sentences.
